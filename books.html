30 Best Teen Romance Books to Read When You're Crushing

In love? There's a book for that.

By Stacey Grant and Leah Campano
Dec 17, 2021
Courtney Chavez

Love and crushes are super confusing. Are your feelings for them genuine? Do they like you-like you? How do you know if you're ready for the next step? While asking your mom, sister, and BFFs is a great go-to, sometimes it's nice to get perspective on your love life from from an unbiased source: YA romance books.

We've compiled a list of some of the best teen romance novels that'll keep you believing in love, even when you're dealing with heartbreak and/or confusion.

Note: We only included books that were stand-alone stories, meaning they're not part of a series. This is why the likes of To All the Boys I've Loved Before, Twilight, and The Hunger Games aren't included on the list. Please don't @ us; we know they're A+ romances.

1 "They Both Die at the End" by Adam Silvera
HaperTeen

The story follows Rufus and Mateo, two boys who recently found out that it is their final day on earth. Fate brings them together in NYC as they try to live their final hours to the fullest before they have to say goodbye forever.

BUY NOW

2 "The Stars and The Blackness Between Them" by Junauda Petrus
Charles Chaisson

Told from two perspectives, this fearless story follows two young Black girls struggling to find their place in the world. Audre is going through a lot. Originally from Trinidad, she’s sent to live with her father in Minneapolis after her mother catches her with a secret girlfriend. In Minneapolis she’ll meet Mabel, who happens to be going through a lot, too. Mabel has been ill for most of the summer and thinks... she might like girls. When their worlds collide, love hits hard and fast — but adding to the momentum is a grim diagnosis that thrusts their romance into an uncertain future.

BUY NOW

3 "The Music of What Happens" by Bill Konigsberg
Scholastic

Max and Jordan are complete opposites of each other, but they can't help but fall in love while working together at Coq Au Vinny, a 1980s-themed food truck, over the summer. They soon have to come face to face with their biggest fears to truly get what they want out of life and each other.

BUY NOW

4 "Opposite of Always" by Justin A. Reynolds
HarperCollins

What if you had the chance to save your significant other? After his girlfriend Kate dies from sickle cell disease, Jack suddenly finds himself back at the moment where they first met over and over again. Will Jack find something in time to be able to help Kate or will he have to live through their relationship all over again knowing how it ends?

BUY NOW

5 "Hot Dog Girl" by Jennifer Dugan
Penguin Random House

Lou Parker sets out to have the most perfect summer of her life, thanks to her new job at Magic Castle Playland. However, as the summer starts, she realizes that nothing is going as planned, including her new job which requires her to dress up as a hot dog, and her crush, who is currently dating the girl who is also playing the park's princess. After learning that Magic Castle Playland is set to close at the end of the summer, Lou must find a way to save the park and her summer before it ends.

BUY NOW

6 "It's Kind of a Funny Story" by Ned Vizzini
Ned Vizzini

Ambitious New York City teenager Craig Gilner is determined to succeed at life - which means getting into the right high school to get the right job. But once Craig aces his way into Manhattan's Executive Pre-Professional High School, the pressure becomes unbearable.

BUY NOW

7 "The Poet X" by Elizabeth Acevedo
Elizabeth Acevedo

This New York Times bestselling novel in verse (a feat, if ever there was one!) centers on Dominican American Xiomara, a young girl growing up in Harlem with her religious immigrant family. Her mom is overbearing in impressing Catholicism on Xio and her twin brother, but Xio spills her soul in the pages of her journal, documenting the dramas and traumas of first-generation life, addressing religion, crises of faith, sexual harassment, and the rush of first love (and lust).

BUY NOW

8 "The Sun is Also a Star" by Nicola Yoon
Ember

Natasha Kingsley is an undocumented immigrant from Jamaica who is about to be deported due to her status. Since she was brought here when she was young, all she's ever known is the United States. As she tries to find a way for her and her family to stay, she meets Daniel who is unhappy with his current life as he tries to continue to please his parents. The two decide to spend the day together and slowly fall in love even though they know their time together is limited.

BUY NOW

9 "Five Feet Apart" by Rachael Lippincott
Simon & Schuster

Seventeen-year-old Stella must stay in a hospital due to her cystic fibrosis. While she finds her life there dull, she soon meets Will, another young cystic fibrosis patient. Despite their instant connection, they two of them cannot be close together because of their illness. As they fall for each other, they must come to terms with their feelings and the illness that is keeping them apart.

BUY NOW

10 "Simon vs. the Homo Sapiens Agenda" by Becki Albertalli
Balzer + Bray

If you missed the groundbreaking movie adaptation, Love, Simon, that came out in 2018, you need to read this poignant book first.

Simon Spier is a closeted gay teen, but when a classmate learns his secret and threatens to out him, Simon is forced to make a tough decision about his future.

BUY NOW

11 "My So Called Bollywood Life" by Nisha Sharma
Nisha Sharma

Bollywood meets Hollywood in this hilarious and lovelorn sendup of classic rom-com Only You. Winnie Mehta’s family psychic has forever told her she would meet the love of her life before she turned eighteen, that his name would start with an R, and that he would give her a bracelet.So of course, Raj is the one, right? Except they broke up. Which foils everything. And when she meets fellow film geek Dev, well, he so does not fit the prophecy. Can Winnie learn to let go of what she believed and take her fate into her own hands? And, more importantly, will she get her perfect Bollywood ending?

BUY NOW

12 "Me and Earl and the Dying Girl" by Jesse Andrews
Me and Earl and the Dying Girl

Me and Earl and The Dying Girl is written from the perspective of Greg Gaines, an awkward high school senior with no “friends” except a “co-worker” Earl, who he makes film parodies with. This book is where Greg documents what happened to him during his senior year when his mother forces him to socialize with his sort-of ex-girlfriend Rachel, who has just been diagnosed with leukemia. Greg and Rachel form a friendship as Greg tries desperately not to get attached to “the dying girl.”

BUY NOW

13 "Let's Talk About Love" by Claire Kann
Swoon Reads

Alice Johnston decides to swear off dating after confessing she's asexual to her girlfriend, only to have her S.O. end things. Everything's going fine until she meets Takumi and begins to develop feelings for him.

BUY NOW

14 "I Believe in a Thing Called Love" by Maurene Goo
Farrar, Straus and Giroux (BYR)

Desi Lee may be great at the whole school thing, but her dating game needs some serious work. After hunky Luca Drakos waltzes into her life, she decides to use her dad's beloved Korean dramas to help with her love life. Naturally, things don't quite go according to plan.

BUY NOW

15 "Flipped" by Wendelin Van Draanen
Random House

A unique aspect to this book is that it's told in two voices, alternating POVs between Bryce Loski and Juli Baker.

Growing up, Juli was into Bryce, but he wasn't into her. But when they're in the eighth grade, the ~romantic~ vibes for the two have, well, flipped.

BUY NOW

16 "Calling My Name" by Liara Tamani
Liara Tamani

Houston teen Taja has always walked the straight and narrow path. She’s smart, pretty, religious, and decidedly not rebellious—until, suddenly, she is. Told in gorgeous, poetic vignettes, this coming of age story tells the tale of Taja’s exploration of faith, first love, sexuality, family, and the ties that both bond and bind. As she sheds the role of obedient daughter for something bolder, will she lose faith and family?

BUY NOW

17 "The Fault in Our Stars" by John Green
Dutton Books

During a support group meeting for kids with cancer, Hazel Grace Lancaster encounters Augustus "Gus" Waters. Together, the two deal with their illnesses while trying to meet the author of Hazel's favorite book in Amsterdam.

BUY NOW

18 "Forever..." by Judy Blume
Bradbury Press

This book caused quite a controversy when it was first published in 1975, due to its explicit sexual content and references to birth control.

Katherine Danziger and Michael Wagner meet at a NYE party and eventually fall in love. They decide to have sex, but will their love last (dare we say it?) forever?

BUY NOW

19 "The Truth About Forever" by Sarah Dessen
Penguin Group Inc.

Macy thinks her summer is going to be super boring while boyfriend Jason is away at camp and she's stuck working a job at the library, but her life gets turned upside down when new opportunities arise. One of those opportunities comes in the form of Wes, an artist with a past.

BUY NOW

20 "Ship It" by Britta Lundin
Freeform

When Claire finally meets an actor named Forest on the show Demon Heart during Comic Con, it's a total dream come true. At least, it is, until Forest laughs off Claire's assertion Forest's character is gay. Considering how Claire's fan fiction involves shipping Forest's character with his male frenemy, this exchange is devastating.

To avoid a PR nightmare after backlash ensues when the Comic Con video goes viral, the show hires Claire to join the cast on their publicity tour, hoping to improve their image with the LGBTQ+ community.

BUY NOW

21 "Eleanor & Park" by Rainbow Rowell
St. Martin's Press

Both Eleanor and Park know first loves rarely ever last, but the 16-year-olds are more than willing to try. The duo form a connection through comic books and '80s music during one school year.

BUY NOW

22 "Everything, Everything" by Nicola Yoon
Random House Children's Books

Due to a rare immunocompromisation, Madeline Whittier has not left her house in seventeen years — severe allergies mean that going outside could be fatal. But then a boy named Olly moves in next door and turns Maddy's world upside down. They start to chat online and through their respective bedroom windows, getting closer until eventually, Maddy is left questioning if Olly is the one to risk it all for.

BUY NOW

23 "What If It's Us" by Becky Albertalli & Adam Silvera
HarperTeen

Arthur is spending the summer in NYC and meets Ben, carrying a box of his ex-boyfriend's things, at the post office. The duo sorta-kinda-maybe strike up a relationship, always asking the question "What if?"

BUY NOW

24 "Red, White & Royal Blue" by Casey McQuiston
St. Martin's Publishing Group

Alex Claremont-Diaz is the First Son of the United States. Prince Henry is British royalty. And they are absolute rivals. But when a public confrontation between the two threatens American/British relations, they are forced to fake a friendship for the press. What begins as a facade soon grows much deeper, as Alex and Henry find themselves falling in love.

BUY NOW

25 "This Is Kind of An Epic Love Story" by Kacen Callender
Kacen Callender

A fresh, charming rom-com perfect for fans of Simon vs. the Homo Sapiens Agenda and Boy Meets Boy, This is Kind of an Epic Love Story is about Nathan Bird, an aspiring screenwriter and a strong opponent of happy endings. After witnessing enough heartbreak in his life, Nate swears love isn’t in the cards for him. That all changes when Nate’s childhood best friend Oliver moves back to town, challenging everything Nate thought he knew about love, friendship, and himself. Ollie is hard-of-hearing, but his abilities are never a central plot point of this novel. In this inclusive, insightful rom-com, Nate and Ollie’s blossoming romance takes center stage, spotlighting two Black and Latino protagonists that strive to defy the odds.

BUY NOW

26 "Today Tonight Tomorrow" by Rachel Lynn Solomon
Rachel Lynn Solomon

Rowan Roth and Neil McNair have been bitter rivals for all of high school, clashing on test scores, student council elections, and even gym class pull-up contests. While Rowan, who secretly wants to write romance novels, is anxious about the future, she'd love to beat her infuriating nemesis one last time.

BUY NOW

27 "I Love You So Mochi" by Sarah Kuhn
Sarah Kuhn's Young

Sarah Kuhn’s sweet debut follows Kimi Nakamura, a first-gen fashionista stuck between her mother’s dreams and her own. Kimi’s mom thinks painting is her daughter’s passion — but Kimi finds herself at the sewing machine more than the canvas. When Kimi is invited to visit her estranged grandparents in Kyoto for spring break, she jumps at the chance to escape the pressure she feels at home. . In Japan, she embarks on a soul-seeking journey rich with culture, hoping to decipher her problems and discover her purpose… and maybe love? Enter Akira, the ambitious city-native who stumbles into her life wearing a giant mochi costume.

BUY NOW

28 "Felix Ever After" by Kacen Callender
HarperCollins Publishers

Felix is Black, queer, transgender, and wants to fall in love. After an anonymous user posts transphobic messages about Felix online, Felix strikes back and launches a catfish mission against the cyberbully. He expected the satisfaction of revenge — definitely not the reality of ending up in a complex love triangle.

BUY NOW

29 "Love from A to Z" by S.K. Ali
Simon & Schuster

Zayneb is suspended from school after confronting her teacher and calling out their Islamophobia. Sent to her aunt's house in Doha, Qatar, for spring break, Zayneb meets Adam on her flight. Adam has been recently diagnosed with multiple sclerosis and must go home to tell his family. Their meet-cute develops into a relationship much deeper, as they begin a love journey rooted in faith and identity.

BUY NOW

30 "A Pho Love Story" by Loan Le
Simon & Schuster

Bao Nguyen and Linh Mai find themselves in an unexpected romance, complicated by their families' years-long competition over their neighboring pho restaurants. The two must confront the question of whether or not their love could survive such a feud.

BUY NOW
